Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the RISE Program

What makes RISE different from traditional advertising?

RISE targets 100% verified travelers with genuine travel intent, eliminating ad waste entirely. Unlike traditional advertising that reaches broad audiences, every RISE impression reaches someone actively flying, with 2-18 hours of uninterrupted engagement time.

How long does it take to see results?

Most destinations begin seeing measurable results within 3-6 months of launch. However, the full impact typically manifests over 12-18 months as content reaches more passengers and brand awareness builds across multiple touchpoints.

What is the typical ROI for RISE Program participants?

Our clients typically see 3.5-5x ROI multipliers, with some achieving even higher returns. The exact ROI depends on factors like destination appeal, airline route alignment, content quality, and overall marketing integration.

Do we need existing content or will you create it?

We work with both scenarios. If you have existing high-quality content, we can adapt it to IFE specifications. If new content is needed, we can coordinate production or connect you with our network of content creators.

Which airlines will feature our destination?

Airline selection is strategic and based on your target markets, existing air access, and growth opportunities. We analyze route networks across our 114 partner airlines to identify the best opportunities for your destination.

How do you measure performance and success?

We track multiple metrics including impressions, engagement time, content interactions, website referrals, and most importantly, incremental visitation attributed to inflight exposure. Monthly reports provide comprehensive performance insights.

Is RISE suitable for small or regional destinations?

Absolutely. RISE is designed to be scalable for destinations of all sizes. We can tailor the program to focus on specific airlines and routes that serve your destination, making it cost-effective even for smaller DMOs.
